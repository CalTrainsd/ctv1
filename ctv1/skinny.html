Dumbbell Military Press

Hold a dumbbell in each hand, just above shoulder-level. Press the weights up until your arms are almost straight. Then slowly lower the dumbbells to the starting position.

cancel

Lateral Raises

Hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing the sides of your body, arms straight. Raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder height. Then slowly return them to the starting position.

cancel

Dumbbell Bicep Curl

Using a palms-up grip with arms extended, curl dumbbell in right hand up as far as possible, then return to the initial position. Repeat with right arm, then switch to your left arm.

cancel

Dips

Inhale and lower your body by bending at the elbows until you lower yourself far enough to where there is an angle slightly smaller than 90 degrees between the upper arm and the forearm, exhale and comback to starting position.

cancel

Preacher Curl

Position your body so that your chest is against the bench and your arms extend over it. Pull the weight up towards your shoulders, squeeze your biceps and slowly release to the starting position.

cancel

Dumbbell Tricep Extension

Hold a dumbbell with both hands at one end behind your head. Stand straight, palms facing up and gripping the dumbbell around the top plate. Raise the dumbbell overhead to arms length, keeping your elbows close to your head during the entire movement. Lower dumbbell to the starting position in a semicircular motion until your forearms touch your biceps.

cancel

Tricep Cable Push Down

Attach a short straight bar to a high cable pulley. Grip the bar palms facing down, hands about 10 inches apart. Keep your body steady and your elbows close to the sides of your body as you push the bar down to the front of your thighs. Squeeze and return to the starting position using the same motion.

cancel

Leg Extension

Sit with your feet under the padded bar and grasp the side handles. Fully extend the legs, then return slowly to the starting position.

cancel

Leg Curl

Lie facedown with your ankles under the pad and grip the handles. Curl your legs up until your hamstrings are fully contracted. Then slowly straighten the legs, lowering them back to the starting position.

cancel

Leg Press

Sit with the knees flexed about 90 degrees and your feet about shoulder-width apart on the platform, toes pointed slightly outward. Extend the legs, then return slowly to starting position.

cancel

Walking Lunges

Stand with a dumbbell in each hand. Stay upright as you step forward with the right leg, bending both knees and descending until the forward thigh is parallel to the floor. Push up and step forward with the other leg and repeat.

cancel

Hip Abductor

Sit on the abduction machine with your thighs parallell to the floor. Push your knees and legs outward as far as you can. Then slowly return to the starting position.

cancel

Chest Press

Lie on the ball keeping your back flat. Hold the dumbbells over your upper chest with your arms straight and positioned a little more than shoulder-width apart. Slowly lower the dumbbells to make slight contact with your upper chest area. Then drive the weight straight up from your chest until your elbows are nearly extended.

cancel

Push Ups

Begin face-down on the floor, balancing on your toes. Position the hands slightly broader than shoulder width apart so your body forms a straight line with your arms extended. Bend your elbows and lower your body until it is nearly touching the floor, then push your body up and back to the starting position.

cancel

Standing Fly

Hold a cable in each hand at shoulder level with arms extended out to the sides of the chest. Keep the arms nearly straight as you push the cables forward and together directly above the chest, palms facing each other. Then move your arms to the starting position and repeat.

cancel

Dumbbell Bent Over Row

Step forward with left leg and bend over at the hips. Grasp a dumbbell in your right hand and place left elbow on your left knee or on a becnh , keeping the lower back almost parallel to the floor and the head facing down. Keep your arm close to the side of your body as you pull the dumbbell up to your side. Squeeze and return to starting position.

cancel

Front Lat Pull Down

Attach a long bar to the lat pull down machine and adjust the kneepads. Hold lat bar with hands about 35 apart. Pull the bar straight down to the top of your chest, then slowly release to the starting position.

cancel

Seated Calf Raises

Sit on a calf raise machine with your knees under the knee pads and your heels hanging off the edge of the platform. Raise your heels straight up as high as possible, hold for a moment and return to the starting position.
